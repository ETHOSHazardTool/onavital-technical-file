What’s in the box

ONAVITAL include 4 differentiated parts:

  1. The monitoring device.

  2. A detachable wrist strap.

  3. The magnetic charge cable.

  4. Button pin.

Getting started

  1. Take the ONAVITAL monitoring device out of the box and activate by charging it.

  2. Charging: The monitoring device should be charged upon first use. To charge the battery attach the magnetic charge cable to the monitoring device and connect it to a 5W to 20W USB charger (not provided). A visual indicator (see Monitoring device visual indicators Section) on the monitoring device will appear when the device is fully charged. A full charge takes between 2-3 hours.

Your credentials.

Once you have purchased your ONAVITAL, you will have received an email to your personal email account from noreply@onalabs.com. Follow the instructions to set-up your credentials. More information on section ONAVITAL Platform.

Setup.

You setup your ONAVITAL using the ONAVITAL APP (available on Android and IOS).

  1. Log-in on the APP using your recently set up credentials.

  2. Bring the smart phone within 3 m (10 ft.) of the monitoring device.

  3. Follow information on section ONAVITAL App.

  4. Your device automatically recognizes ONAVITAL each time it is activated and in range.

Placement and Operation.

Once the device has been activated, charged, and paired, attach the wrist stap on the monitoring device and place the ONAVITAL monitoring device on your non-dominant hand.

The correct positioning is as follows:

  1. Fasten your ONAVITAL bracelet around your wrist with the sensor facing toward the skin and the lettering facing you.

  2. The device should be positioned 2 cm above the scaphoid bone as indicated.

  1. The sensor should be placed in direct contact with the skin.

  2. Tighten the bracelet to be snug but comfortable to ensure that the bracelet does not move around while using your hands.

ONAVITAL have been designed for ease of use and automation, so no more actions are needed for a proper operation of the device (Only exception is the actions required for On Demand measurements). The monitoring device will autonomously identify when the user is wearing it and provide a visual indicator (see Monitoring device visual indicators Section) .

The monitoring device will take measurements every 15 minutes without any need for interaction from the user.

It can be used 24 hours a day even when taken a shower. And it will provide a visual indicator (see Monitoring device visual indicators Section) when the monitoring device needs to be charged.

On Demand Measurements.

By means of a button on the APP, the user can do an on demand measurement. This kind of measurement is meant to provide a really accurate measurement of Blood Pressure. To do so, the user must follow these indications:

  1. Sit with your back straight and your feet flat on the floor, ensuring your legs are not crossed.

  2. Rest your hand palm-side down on a flat surface, such as a desk or table.

  3. Keep your arm muscles relaxed and still throughout the measurement process, which lasts for 1 minute.

  4. Remain calm, still, and silent

  5. The device will indicate that is doing an on-demand measurement with a visual indicator (green light).

  6. You will know that the On Demand measurement is finished when the green light is off.

Monitoring device button manipulation.

On the side of the monitoring device is a small hole to activate an internal button. This button can be pressed using the button pin. There are three types of interactions:

  • Single push of the button: This will reset the pairing so you can connect to your phone.

  • Press the button during 10 seconds: This will put your device to sleep mode in order to be stored.

  • Wake up the device: If the device is charged and in sleep mode, press the button until you see the visual indicator from the device looking for a connection.

Monitoring device visual indicators.

ONAVITAL will provide different visual indicators for the user to know the status of the device. Here is a list of these visual indicators.

  • Blue light – The device is charging.

  • Green light – If connected to the charger, the device is fully charged. If doing an On Demand measurement, green light will be on as the measurement is taking place. Please follow the instructions of the On Demand measurement.

  • Blue light and green light blinking - Waiting for the connection with the APP. No measurements will be done until the connection is stablished.

  • Blue light blinking – The device is checking user presence (the user has put on the monitoring device).

  • Green light during 5 seconds after blue light blinking – The device has found the user and has activated.

  • Red light blinking – The battery level is low. Charge the device.

  • No light signal – The device is working normally.

  • Constant red light – The device is not working normally.

Offline measurements.

If you are not connected to the APP due to any circumstace, such as moving more than 10 meters away from your phone or not having the bluetooth activated, the vital signs measurements will be stored on the internal flash memory.

All data stored in the internal flash memory will be automatically uploaded to your APP once the device is reconnected to the APP via bluetooth.

ONAVITAL Application.

The application is intended only for the use of the user, since this is the gateway or the device that is used for the acquisition of the data from the monitoring device, and the sending of this data to the cloud.

Permissions of the APP

  • The application will request permissions for: location, connections to nearby devices, running the app in the background and send notifications. These four permissions are necessary for the correct functioning of the APP.

 NOTE: For Android versions earlier than 10, not all these permissions will be requested.

Initial Screen

Login

Using the user credentials previously configured, log in.

NOTE. - Make sure you have the Bluetooth (BLE) of the mobile activated.

Reset Password

  • By clicking in the ‘Forgot your password?’ button, a pop-up is displayed.

  • By clicking ‘Continue’ the app will redirect you to the web page where you can change the password.

Informed Consent, Privacy Policy and Terms and Conditions Screen

If is the first time you enter to the application, you will be redirected to the permissions screen.

By clicking in the three dots, you can contact support or logout.

The following permissions need to be accepted to access the application functionalities.

  • Informed Consent.- By pressing the permissions you can access to the information of the Informed onsent.

  • Privacy Policy and Terms and Conditions.- By pressing the permissions you can access to the web where the information is displayed.

If the permissions are not accepted and you press ‘Continue’, an error will be displayed in the screen.

Once the permissions have been read and accepted, press ‘Continue’.

Connection setup screen

Once you have accepted all the permissions you will be redirected to the Connection setup screen.

In this screen you can see the historic without connecting the device, contact support or pair the device.

Contact support

By clicking the 'Support' option in the app bar menu. You will be redirected to the default email with a template to be filled.

See historic without connecting the device

By selecting the option 'Home' in the app bar menu. You will be redirected to the Home screen and access the historic of each parameter, by clicking each card.

There are three options to see the historic:

  • Last 24 hours

  • Last week

  • Last month

Pair ONAVITAL device

On this screen the pairing of ONAVITAL with the mobile is carried out.

NOTE: The pair code that appears in the screen is instantly copied.

Pairing process events
Cancel Pairing Process

Once the ‘Pair Device’ button has been clicked, you can cancel the process by clicking on the ‘Cancel’ button.

Pairing not successful

A pairing can fail:

  • The pair code is not entered in the pop-up (Bluetooth pairing request) displayed in the mobile.

    • To solve this problem, press ‘Pair Device’ and when the pop-up is displayed, paste the pair code.

  • The device has lost the pairing but in the mobile it’s still registered as bonded.

    • Unpair device from mobile before trying again the pairing process.

Pairing success

Start pairing process by clicking ‘Pair Device’.

Paste the pair code in the pop-up (Bluetooth pairing request). Finally, press ‘Pair’ and wait for the pairing process to end.

After a few seconds when the pairing is successful, the application will be redirected to the home screen.

Connection setup errors

No device assigned in database

When the user does not have a device assigned to the platform or has several devices assigned to the platform,the following error will be displayed.
To solve this error contact the administrator to make the respective modifications to the platform.

No pair code assigned to device or user is not assigned to a group

When the device does not have the pair code configured on the platform or when the user does not belong to a group in the platform, the following error will be displayed.

So, the pairing process cannot start.
To solve this error, contact the administrator to make the respective corrections on the platform.

Home screen

In this screen by clicking the three dots you will be able to contact support and logout.

Connection states

Searching state: In the ‘Searching state’, the application searches for the paired device to connect via BLE.

Connecting state: In the "connecting state", the connection is being stablished.

Connected state: In the "connected state", the connection has been stablished.

BLE powered off state

When the BLE from the mobile is disabled, an advertisement is displayed. The measurement won’t arrive until the BLE is enabled.

Measurements states

The measurements are displayed in two forms:

  • Coloured card: The signal received by the ONAVITAL device is fine, and the value is sent to the platform.

  • Gray card: The signal received by the ONAVITAL device is bad, so the data is not sent to the platform.

On Demand Measure

The user can trigger a measurement by clicking on the ‘Measure’ button.

The state of the button will change to 'Measuring' until the process is finished.

IMPORTANT! For the On-Demand Measurement, please follow the instructions explained on the previous section entitled On Demand Measurement.

View History Data

By clicking each card of each signal vital, you can see the history for: Last 24 hrs, Last week and Last month.

Low battery indicator

When the device has low battery an alarm/indicator will be displayed.

The user will only use the platform to configure the new password or reset the password.

  • When a User is registered using an email account as credential, an email is sent to that user.

NOTE: If pressing the Verify Account button opens the browser and indicates that the invitation has expired, contact the Organization Administrator.

  • The Verify Account button on the Email sent you to a Password confirmation tab on the browser.

  • Once the user fill the Password confirmation, the user gets the notification of the Password confirmation.

  • If the user clicks again in the Verify Account button from the Email sent, they will be sent again to the Password Confirmation tab. However, if they want to change their password again, using this link, they will be denied.

  • To Reset the Password, the user must go to the Portal found here https://organization.app.cloud-onalabs.com/auth/login/main and click on the Password Reset link.

  • Once the user click on the link, the user will be sent to the Reset Password tab, where they will introduce their email address. Once they click on the green button, an email will be sent to them.

  • The user will receive an Email to Reset their Password clicking on the Reset button.

  • That button will send the user to the Password Reset Portal where the user can set the new Password.

  • Once a new Password is set, the user will be communicated of the successful completion. And if the APP session is still running on the older credentials, it will be closed, so the new Password needs to be introduced.

View history

  • Click on the User tab and select the user. A box in the righ side will be displayed.

  • Click on EXPAND, and you will be redirected to a new screen with all the information from the user.

  • Click on the Measurements tab, which lead you to the screen where the graphs are shown. You are in the History tab.

  • Select the start date of the history, first set the date and then the hour. Then click Apply.

  • Select the view range, this means the time range that you want to display in the graph starting from the start date. Finally, the history data is displayed.

Physiological Data, Metrics and Visualization.

Whenever you wear ONAVITAL, it monitors your Vital Signs and more. It features the following metrics:

  1. Pulse Rate (Beats per minute).

  2. Oxygen Saturation (percentage of oxygenated hemoglobin).

  3. Arterial blood pressure, both systolic and diastolic (millimeters of mercury above surrounding atmospheric pressure).

  4. Skin temperature (celsius).

After it is paired, the monitoring device is able to send you Vital Signs data to your ONAVITAL account whenever the monitor is awake and within range of your smartphone. You can visualize a summary of the data by day, week, or months through the ONAVITAL APP.

Monitoring device troubleshooting

  • If you pick up the device after charging it and do not indicate with the visual indicators that it is looking for user presence or connection with the app (see section of Monitoring Device Visual Indications), please do a complete device reset by putting the device to sleep and waking it up as indicated on the Monitoring Device Button section.

  • If the APP do not find your device and no visual indicator from the monitoring device is present, please do a complete device reset by putting the device to sleep and waking it up as indicated on the Monitoring Device Button section.

Considerations

Contraindications

  • People without the skills and competencies to operate the digital solution.

  • Pulse oximeters have limitations and a risk of inaccuracy under certain circumstances. In many cases, the level of inaccuracy may be small and not clinically meaningful; however, there is a risk that an inaccurate measurement may result in unrecognized low oxygen saturation levels. Due to accuracy limitations at the individual level, SpO2 provides more utility for trends over time instead of absolute thresholds.

  • Many patient factors may also affect the accuracy of the measurement. The most current scientific evidence shows that there are some accuracy differences in pulse oximeters between dark and light skin pigmentation; this difference is typically small at saturations above 80% and greater when saturations are less than 80%.

  • Wireless pulse oximeters do not have unlimited range. To work effectively the wireless oximeter must be in Bluetooth range of the device they are communicating with.

  • Do not use with neonatal patients.

  • If the device is mechanically damaged it must not be used and must be disposed.

  • The device have been designed and evaluated to be used in adults, do not use on pedriatics.

  • Do not use with patients with significant deformity, swelling, irritation, degenerative changes or edema of
    the wrist.

  • Do not use the wrist monitor on patients with localized infection, ulceration or skin lesions involving the wrist.

  • Do not use the wrist monitor on patients that have restricted blood flow e.g. tourniquet, pressure cuff or IV line.

  • Do not use with patients with tremors or convulsions.

  • Do not use with patients with peripheral vascular disease affecting the hands.

  • Do not use on an area with a tattoo.

  • DO NOT use in MRI or a CT environment.

Precautions

  • Disposal of this device should be performed in accordance with local regulations.

  • Please do not disassemble any system components, except the detachable strap. This system is not intended for user servicing. Any maintenance or repairs, including battery replacement, should be performed exclusively by authorized personnel. For assistance, please contact Onalabs customer support.

  • Use the device only for the purpose described in the indications for use.

  • Do not use accessories which are not supplied or recommended by the manufacturer.

  • Do not use the device if it is not working properly or if it has suffered any damage, for example, a damaged casing, or damage caused by dropping the equipment or immersing it in water or other liquids. Stop using the device and contact the manufacturer.

  • The device is designed to determine the percentage of arterial oxygen saturation of functional hemoglobin. Factors that may degrade performance or affect the accuracy of the measurement include the following:

    • Device not applied correctly

    • Excessive motion

    • Intravascular dyes

  • Avoid too much light such as sunlight or bright indoor lighting.

  • The device has no audible alarms and is intended for periodic spot checking.

  • The wrist-monitor may not work when circulation is reduced. Warm or rub the wrist area to increase
    perfusion.

  • The device does not require cleaning on a routine basis. However, if the outer surface of the device is not clean, it may be cleaned with a soft, lint-free cloth moistened by ethyl alcohol (70-85%) until visually clean.

  • Do not sterilize, autoclave, or immerse this device in liquid.

  • This device is a precision electronic instrument and must be repaired by Onalabs Inno-Hub SL qualified technical professionals. Field repair of the device is not possible. Do not attempt to open the case or repair the electronics. Opening the case may damage the device and void the warranty.

  • The equipment complies with IEC60601-1-2 for electromagnetic compatibility for medical electrical
    equipment and / or systems. This standard is designed to provide reasonable protection against harmful
    interference in a typical medical installation. However, because of the proliferation of radio frequency
    transmitting equipment and other sources of electrical noise in health care and other environments, it is possible that high levels of interference due to close proximity or strength of a source might disrupt the some of the features of this device.

  • Portable and mobile RF communications equipment including CT, MRI, diathermy, RFID, and electronic
    article security systems can affect medical electrical equipment.

  • Follow local, state, and national governing ordinances and recycling instructions regarding disposal or recycling of the device and device components, including batteries.

  • In compliance with the European Directive on Waste for Electrical and Electronic equipment (WEEE) 2002/96/ EC, do not dispose of this product as unsorted municipal waste. This device contains WEEE materials; please contact your distributor regarding return or recycling of the device.

Warnings

  • Real-world accuracy may differ from accuracy in the lab setting. While reported accuracy is an average of all patients in the test sample, there are individual variations among patients. The SpO2 reading should always be considered an estimate of oxygen saturation. For example, if a pulse oximeter reads 90%, then the true oxygen saturation in the blood is generally between 86-94%. Pulse oximeter accuracy is highest at saturations of 90-100%, intermediate at 80-90%, and lowest below 80%.

  • The SPO2 and ABP vital signs are highly sensitive to the location of the device on the patient and any movement from the patient. ONAVITAL have an embedded analyser for this eventualities so if any measurement is influenced by placement or movement, ONAVITAL is going to discard these measurements for safety reasons.

  • Blood pressure is a dynamic physiological parameter that can fluctuate throughout the day in response to various factors such as body position and physical activity among other factors that usually affect blood pressure like stress, medication, etc. Therefore, the automated blood pressure monitoring may not always reflect an individual's true blood pressure status on that precise moment. Automated blood pressure monitoring data is intended as a general temporal overview through time. For accurate blood pressure measurements please follow-up the instructions fo the On Demand measurements.

  • Interpretation of blood pressure readings should consider the patient's overall clinical condition, medical history, and other hemodynamic parameters to accurately assess cardiovascular health.

  • The temperature of the applicable part may exceed 41ºC during operation. Please handle with care to avoid burns or injury.
