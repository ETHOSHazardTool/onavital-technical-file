1. Purpose

To provide a rationale for the classifying software as a medical device in accordance with Annex IIX ofthe Regulation (EU) 2017/745 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 5 April 2017 on medical devices, amending Directive 2001/83/EC, Regulation (EC) No 178/2002 and Regulation (EC) No 1223/2009 classification rules.

2. Rationale

In the EU, MD are classified into four classes: I, IIa, IIb and III. The classification is a risk-based system which takes into account the potential risks to patients, associated with MD technical design and manufacturing. The classification rules described in Annex VIII of MDR, are a set of criteria that can be combined in various ways to determine device’s classification. Establishing the proper MD’s class is essential for defining the strategy and studies to be carried out for its eventual commercialization. Below it is described the classification for each component of the product.

By definition, the product is considered to be an active device, however the intended use and specifically whether there is a designated medical purpose are taken into account when applying classification rules to the medical device.

ONAVITAL is:

  • standalone, non-invasive, active.

  • does not come into contact with injured skin; and

  • intended to be used directly for the medical benefit of individual patients.

  • is not used in conjunction with another medical device or accessory.

  • is intended to monitor vital physiological parameters.

  • is well-established technology.

2.1 Hardware (HW)

According to the nature of the wrist band and its intended purpose, it would be classified as IIa or IIb using rule X depending on the final intended purpose:

  • Class IIa: “Active devices intended for diagnosis or monitoring of vital physiological
    processes”.

  • Class IIb: “Active devices monitoring of vital physiological parameters and the nature of
    variations of those parameters is such that it could result in immediate danger to the patient”.
    For examples, variations on the activity of the central nervous, system, respiration or cardiac
    performance.

According to the intended use, claims, intended use environment and indications, the hardware is classified as Class IIb medical device accordingly to Rule 10.

2.2 Software (SW)

The first aspect to be addressed when classifying a SW is to determine if the SW can be categorized
as a MD or not. SW can be qualified as follows according to the Medical Device Coordination Group (MDCG) Guidance on Qualification and Classification of Software in Regulation (EU) 2017/745 –MDR and Regulation (EU) 2017/746 –IVDR (thereafter called MDCG 2019 Software Guideline):
• Medical Device Software (MDSW): the SW fulfils with definition of a MD, meaning that it
has a medical purpose.
• SW driving or influencing the use of a MD: SW which has not a medical purpose itself but
helps on the functioning of a MD.
• Health-related SW: SW without a medical purpose, and not considered a MD nor regulated
under the MDR.


Onalabs SW is used to transmit the information coming from the hardware and display and store information related with the vital signs of the patient. It functions as a medical data delivery system and has no direct medical purpose and is therefore presented as an accessories to a medical device.

3.Classification

According to the intended use, claims, intended use environment and indications, the hardware is classified as Class IIa MD.
