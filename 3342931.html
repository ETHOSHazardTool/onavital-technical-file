1. Purpose

To describe the process for managing the ONAVITAL v2.0.0 lifecycle including Early Design and Planning, Development, Implementation, Test & Validations, Certify and Release.

2. Scope

This procedure describes the steps needed, and the control mechanisms in place, to ensure that ONAVITAL v2.0.0 meet the requirements for function, performance, safety and applicable statutory and regulatory requirements.

3. Responsibilities

The Quality Representative is responsible for ensuring records are maintained and aligned to any quality and regulatory requirements.

It is the responsibility of each development team to develop Products and Services in accordance with this procedure.

It is Management’s responsibility to provide adequate resources to manage the Products and Services' lifecycles in accordance with this procedure.

4. Definitions

Description of the Design Phases within this document:

Term / Acronym

Description

Early Design

Proof that the concept of the Product and/or Service is achievable

Planning

Proof that the Product and/or Service can deliver the requirements of the market

Delivery of the Product and/or Service to the market in a manner that meets the necessary regulatory landscape

5. Procedure

Inputs relating to product requirements are determined and records maintained.

These inputs  include:

  • Functional and non-functinal requirements.

  • Applicable regulatory requirements.

  • Applicable output(s) of risk management; OV Part E: Risk Management Files .

  • As appropriate, information derived from previous designs.

  • Other requirements essential for design and development of the product and processes.

Requirements, as recorded in OV C3.1. Requirements Specification are complete, unambiguous, able to be verified or validated, and not in conflict with each other. The following requirements are captured:

  • Requirements that address the intended use.

  • Interface / user interface requirements.

  • Requirements that address user population and environments of use.

  • Safety and security requirements.

  • Requirements for accompanying documents such as instructions for use.

  • Requirements for support (Upgrades from previous versions, rollback to the previous version after upgrade, timely security patches and updates, de-commisioning, irreversible deletion, transfer and/or retention of data

5.1 Early Design

Following conceptual investigations, a Business Plan is prepared that indicates the commercial feasibility of the new product or service.

The detail of the overall project to meet the requirements of the Business Plan and the quality and regulatory requirements is detailed in the OL QMS-MAN001- Quality Manual. It details the specifications required for the initiation of new project.

Completion of the Early Design phase is determined as the point at which a Bussiness Plan and Product User Requirements have been stablished.

5.2 Planning

The Planning phase initiates with the establishment of a Development Team that develops a Work Plan (developed here: https://onalabs.atlassian.net/jira/software/c/projects/OV/boards/6) based on the inputs from the Early Design phase.

The Development Team does a first approach planning to the design requirements specifications (Design Requirements Specifications, found here: OV C3.1. Requirements Specification). The various specifications may not contain all of the required information at the beginning of the project; however as the project progresses the Development Team must ensure that the specifications are updated to include as much information as necessary.

During the planning phase are also considered all the diferent Release versions according to OLQMS-WI002 - Product version and documentation . The release versions can be found here: OV A1.6. Product History Record.

Also, a Risk Management Plan and Risk Assessment is developed, found here: OV Part E: Risk Management Files.

In addition, during the planning phase, the following is considered:

  • The essential elements of the OL QMS-MAN001- Quality Manual have been determined.

  • There is a clearly defined process in place to deliver the project and commercialisation goals.

  • Planning for the eventual deployment/manufacture begins and deployment/manufacturing processes are investigated for suitability.

5.3 Development

During the Development phase the Work Plan is executed and the Design Requirements refined to create the different release versions planned and complying with the specifications documented in the OL QMS-MAN001- Quality Manual.

During the Development stage, the Development team work in two-week sprints managed by Jira Software, found here: https://onalabs.atlassian.net/jira/software/projects/FYS/boards/1.

The Development technical files for Firmware and Software repository is found here: https://github.com/orgs/OnalabsInnoHub/dashboard

The Development technical files for Hardware and Housing repository is found on confluence.

During this phase, critical changes on the design are controlled using OLQMS-PRO013 - Change Management SOP. Also, documents are created to capture the specifications that fulfil the requirements of the early design and planning stages before the design output documents are finalised.

A first set of design and development verification/validation test protocols are prepared to prove the design solutions are capable of meeting the requirements of the specifications (see OV Part F: Pre-Clinical Information and OV C3.1. Requirements Specification). Design and development outputs need to be useful for verification against the design and development inputs and shall be approved prior to release. This requires that design outputs:

  • Meet the input requirements for development.

  • Provide appropriate information for implementation.

  • Contain or reference product acceptance criteria.

The employees involved on the project agree the technical design and approach before completing the development task. Tasks are allocated to the developers by creating tasks and sub-tasks. As the task progresses, it is moved from “To Do” to the “In Progress” category.

When completed the task owner moves the task from “In Progress” to the “Pending Review” category. The testers are then responsible for testing and/or regression testing the task before moving it to the “Done” category.

This Task advancement is documented on the Requirements Specifications, found here: OV C3.1. Requirements Specification. Clinical investigations must be done, if necessary, to validate the different development items.

A risk assessment, found here: OV Part E: Risk Management Files, is done to ensure the all the relevant controls are in place to ensure the product presents no unacceptable risks to the potential user and others during validation including clinical trials.

During this stage, the device will be calibrated according to the following protocols:

  • Calibration protocols.

Found here: OV F7. Calibration

5.4 Implementation

The implementation phase consists of the transition from the Development MVP to an industrializable/beta version of the product, for subsequent testing and validation.

During this phase, the production/industrialization engineering is done and the Product Quality Agreements with the Suppliers, for manufacturing and assembly, must be approved by both parties.

From this production/industrialization engineering work with the manufacturing and assemly Suppliers, the final version of the technical documentation is completed (see: Detailed Information to be supplied by Onalabs Inno-hub about the Product, found here: OV Part B: Information Supplied by the Manufacturer and Detailed Design Specifications and Documentation, found here OV Part C: Design and Manufacturing Information).

In addition, a set of Test and Validation Protocols are established to assure the Product quality and safety. These Protocols encompass the following scenarios:

  • Pre-clinical evaluation protocols.

Found here: OV Part F: Pre-Clinical Information

  • Manufacturing and assembly protocols and tests.

Found here: OV C3.4. Product Manufacturing Definitions and Procedures

  • Clinical evaluation plan.

Found here: OV Part G: Clinical Evaluation, PMS and PMCF

5.5 Test, Validation and Verification

Once the implementation has been completed, a release version of the product (see here: OV A1.6. Product History Record) is subjected to the verification and validation protocols previously stablished.

Tests and validations are documented in accordance with company QMS, and the following test objectives have been considered: confidence building, defect detection and reduction of quality risks.

An evaluation plan, complete with clear business, technical and clinical acceptance criteria must be documented prior to commencing the evaluation of the different release versions.

When changes are made during testing and validation, additional tests are performed to verify the effectiveness of the change in correcting the problem and demonstrate that unintended side effects have not been introduced.

Formal approvals are required to exit test and validation. Evaluation plan must be regularly reviewed to assess the usefulness of the current processes in place, what elements need improving and which techniques can be used to improve the processes.

Participants in such reviews include a member of the MRB, the quality representative and product developers. The purpose of the review is to:

  • Evaluate the ability of the evaluation plan metrics and outputs to assess if the product requirements have been met.

  • Identify changes and propose necessary actions.

The reports of the reviews include the identification of the product under review, any necessary actions, the participants involved and the date of the review .

Reports for pre-clinical information regarding Performance and Safety can be found here: OV Part F: Pre-Clinical Information

Reports for clinical evaluation can be found here: OV Part G: Clinical Evaluation, PMS and PMCF

5.6 Certify & Release

5.5.1 Certification

Once all the documentation and evidence have been collected on the Technical File and Key Records, the Product will be submitted for certification (MDR, FDA, etc.)

5.5.2 Release as a Pre-Commercial Product

Once the approval for exit test and validation have been issued. The product is ready for release.

All the elements and processes of the supply chain have been tested and validated, and all the Agreements with the suppliers are in place.

The manufacturing and assembly testing are in place, and all the needed tooling is provided by Onalabs Inno-Hub SL, accordingly to the QMS.

The orders for manufacturing, assembly and package are sent according to the procedures describes in OV C3.4. Product Manufacturing Definitions and Procedures.
