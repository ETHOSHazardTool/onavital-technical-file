2. Similar Devices

This document aims to establish and document a comparison evaluation between ONAVITAL and the similar devices identified through the clinical evaluation process (see documents in OV G1. Clinical Evaluation).

None of these devices has been considered an equivalent product as Onalabs Inno-Hub SL does not have full access to their technical or clinical documentation. However, these devices are considered similar to the product under evaluation for this clinical evaluation process.

The term ‘similar devices’ may be understood as devices belonging to the same generic device group. The MDR defines this as a set of devices having the same or similar intended purposes or a common technology allowing them to be classified in a generic manner not reflecting specific characteristics.

The data from similar devices has been used in the clinical evaluation for the following purposes, in accordance with MDCG 2020-5:

  1. Ensuring that the risk management system is comprehensive by identifying relevant hazards and clinical risks: based on the bibliographic search and on adverse events in vigilance database.

  1. Understanding the state of the art, the natural course of disease and alternative available treatment options: based on the instructions for use and on the bibliographic search.

  1. Helping to define the scope of the clinical evaluation, by identifying any design features in similar devices that pose special performance or safety concerns: based on the bibliographic search and on adverse events in vigilance database.

  1. Provide input for clinical investigation design or post-market clinical follow-up design, and the post-market surveillance system: based on adverse events in vigilance database.

  1. Identification of relevant and specified clinical outcome parameters for the intended clinical benefits, based on the published clinical data pertaining to the similar device(s): based on the bibliographic search .

  1. To define minimum requirements for a quantified clinical benefit that is considered clinically relevant, and/or to identify acceptable occurrence rates of risks and adverse events: based on the bibliographic search and on adverse events in vigilance database.

 A modified table based on Annex I of MDCG 2020-5 has been used for the comparison to clearly demonstrate that these devices are considered similar to ONAVITAL. Table in section 4 is provided as a summary of the content of this document.
