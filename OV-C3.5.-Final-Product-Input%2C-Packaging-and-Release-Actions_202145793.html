1. Purpose

To document the steps for ONAVITAL transit instructions.

2. Scope

These instructions details how ONAVITAL product is received in Onalabs facilities and how it is tested to be labelled as released product. It also details the quality checks to manage potential human error (see also: OV F10.100 Product Release - Validation & Verification)

3. Final Product Input Processing description

Product Input

As defined  in the product manufacturing process (found here: OV C3.4. Product Manufacturing Definitions and Procedures) and in the Quality Agreement signed with the company Oscatech, the final product will enter our system with the required quality documentation and referenced by a production batch number and serial number. These are referenced by the corresponding UDI defined here: https://onalabs.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/ONALABSQMS/pages/3342439/OV+A1.3.+General+Administrative+Information#6.UDI-(unique-device-identifier). This documentation will be sent by e-mail to j.punter@onalabs.com.

Oscatech will package the product to be transported to Onalabs facilities in distribution boxes. The information related to these boxes can be found on:

PE-ONA001-00.pdf

To avoid damages on the product or packaging, it will be checked visually as explained below:

  1. Check for any damages on the product transport packaging. Check that the Batch number on the distribution box documentation is the same one that it is on the documentation sent by mail.

  1. Open the distribution box and extract every package.

  2. Check for all the individual packages and make sure that all of the Batch manufactured products are present.

  3. Visually check every individual package for any damage.

  4. The products are deposited in our offices warehouse until distributors place their orders. Our products do not need any enviornmental control.

Product Quality Documentation Check

All the Quality Documentation and Testing output must be reviewed and accepted by the Technical Manager before further action can be done.

4. Final product release process

Products to be released needs to pass a release process. This process consists on randomly selecting a 1% of the total stock intended to be released and performing the following checks/testings.

Content and Packaging Validation Check

Before placing the product on a transport box, an output testing will be done. This test consists on visually checking every product for any external packaging damage. In addition, 1% of the products to be put on the transport box will be internally checked for any possible packaging error:

  • Open the product packaging and check that no component is missing

ONAVITAL Device with velcro strap attached and and Power Supply Cable must be present

Strap must be securely attached with pin

  • Check that the product packaging labelling UDI match the product housing UDI.

Final product testing

A final testing of the product will be done to 1% of the manufactured devices according to the protocol found here: OV C3.5.1 Product Release Testing

Documentation

The employee doing the inspection will register on the inventory the Checking results with the associated Product Output Batch number and date of the checking. Information about this check must also be put here and accepted by the Technical Manager: OV F10.100 Product Release - Validation & Verification

If everything is correct, the remaining product will be considered released product and added to the stock as such.

5. Product storage.

The products are deposited in our offices warehouse until distributors place their orders. Our products do not need any enviornmental control.

In order to keep proper control of the product stock and storage, please follow these instructions: OLQMS-PRO017 - Warehouse SOP
