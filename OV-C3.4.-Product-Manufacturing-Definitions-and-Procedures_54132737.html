4. Definitions

Manufacturing Batch ID

Manufacturing Batches identify the parts manufactured to be part of the product but not the product itself.

It is identified with the following ManBatchID code: 1YYYYMMDDX

where 1 is the internal product code, YYYY are the digits of the year, MM the month and DD the day of the manufacturing order (eg. electronics manufactured by P4Q), and X is the hexadecimal number of the batch in ascending order (0,1,2,…,F) for each batch manufactured the same day. This only applies to the Electronic components, as we have defined a 1000 units batch, for the rest of components the X will always be 0, as the whole order will be considered a Batch.

This Manufacturing Batch ID will identify the orders on OV C3.3. Product Orders and Receipt of Goods.

Serial Number

Serial numbers identify every single unit as a unique id for each device.

Serial number will be stored on the internal memory of the electronic, printed as a datamatrix code on the housing and on the packaging.

It is identified with the following code: ManBatchIDAAA

where AAA identifies each single device of the Batch (000,001,002,…,998,999)

The serial number of each ONAVITAL is simplified due to the limited area of this board. This serial number is the following one:

1YYMMDDAAA

where 1 is the internal product code, YY are the last two digits of the year, MM the month and AAA identifies each single device of the Batch (000,001,002,…,998,999).

Product Batch ID

It is identified with the following ProdBatchID code: 1NNNYYMM

where 1 is the internal product code, NNN is the batch number in ascending order, YY are the last two digits of the year and MM the month of the manufacturing order.

This Product Batch ID will identify the finished ONAVITAL devices on OV C3.3. Product Orders and Receipt of Goods.

Testing.

6. Processes description.

Order

First of all, Onalabs places an order with LabCircuits for PCB manufacturing, and P4Q for PCB assembly. LabCircuits and P4Q provide a series of dates for both processes, and then Onalabs and P4Q establish the Manufacturing Batch ID and Serial Numbers of the product.

Next, an order is placed on Oscatech for case manufacturing, product assembly, final product testing, labelling, and packaging. The other parts for the final product assembly and packaging are shipped from Onalabs to Oscatech on an agreed date. In addition, the Product Batch ID is stablished on the order to be included on the UDI.

The placement of orders are carried out as described here: OLQMS-PRO005 - Purchasing & Supplier Management SOP

Orders documentation is controlled by the different Batch ID here: OV C3.3. Product Orders and Receipt of Goods

PCB Manufacturing

On the date established on the order placed with LabCircuits, the PCBs are manufactured according to the specifications agreed on the Quality Agreement, found here: OLQMS-REC001.3 - Quality Agreements.

Manufacturing processes in LabCircuits facilities are described here:

Shipping to P4Q

The finnished batch of PCBs are packaged by LabCircuits as agreed on the Quality Agreement, found here: OLQMS-REC001.3 - Quality Agreements. The packaged batch is sent to P4Q for PCB assembly.

PCB Assembly

On the date of manufacturing stablished on the order placed with P4Q, the PCB is assembled according to the specifications agreed on the Quality Agreement, found here: OLQMS-REC001.3 - Quality Agreements.

Assembly processes in P4Q facilities are described here:

Battery Soldering

A lithium polymer battery is soldered to each manufactured board according to the he specifications agreed on the Quality Agreement, found here: OLQMS-REC001.3 - Quality Agreements.

Serial Number Generation

Once the PCBs have been manufactured, a Serial Number is associated to each manufactured board by attaching a sticker to it. This sticker contains the serial number encoded inside a QR code for traceability for product traceability.

JTAG Testing

PCBs are tested using the protocol found here: OV C3.4.1 Hardware Testing

If the test is not passed, P4Q communicates with Onalabs Inno-Hub SL as accorded on the Quality Agreement (OLQMS-REC001.3 - Quality Agreements). The, Onalabs raises a NC form and/or CAPA if it is necessary.

If the test is passed, P4Q delivers the necessary documentation to Onalabs Inno-Hub SL as accorded on the Quality Agreement (OLQMS-REC001.3 - Quality Agreements).

Firmware Flashing

Once the testing have been finished, the final Production Firmware (found here: ) is embedded on the Electronics. This is the final part to finish the production of the Electronic part of the product.

Shipping to Oscatech

The finnished batch of Electronics is packaged by P4Q as agreed on the Quality Agreement, found here: OLQMS-REC001.3 - Quality Agreements. The packaged batch will be sent to Oscatech for final product assembly.

OV Parts Collection

Oscatech receive from Onalabs Inno-Hub SL all the differents parts needed for the final product assembly as agreed on the Quality Agreement found here: OLQMS-REC001.3 - Quality Agreements, and on the date agreed on the Order Placement.

All the parts are visually inspected for packaging damage and other deteriorations. If any deteriorations are observed, Oscatech communicates with Onalabs Inno-Hub SL as accorded on the Quality Agreement (OLQMS-REC001.3 - Quality Agreements). Onalabs raises a NC form and/or CAPA if necessary.

Injection of Plastic Pieces

On the Date of Manufacturing stablished on the Order placed the Plastic Case Pieces are manufactured by Oscatech according to the specifications agreed on the Quality Agreement, found here: OLQMS-REC001.3 - Quality Agreements

Acquisition of Serial Number and UDI Generation

For traceability purposes, Oscatech acquires every Serial Number that is attached on each PCB. An UDI (as described here: OV A1.3. General Administrative Information) is generated for each individual product to be engraved as a DataMatrix on the case (OV C3.2.9. Plastic case and fabric straps) and labelling (OV B.2.2. Labelling ).

Assembly procedure

The final product is assembled according to the instructions described on the quality agreement found here: OLQMS-REC001.3 - Quality Agreements

Final product testing

Final product is tested using the procedures described on the quality agreement found here: OLQMS-REC001.3 - Quality Agreements Final product testing protocol can also be found here: https://onalabs.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/~64060eb0f00d095406f44f19/pages/229802209

If the test is not passed, Oscatech communicates with Onalabs Inno-Hub SL as accorded on the Quality Agreement (OLQMS-REC001.3 - Quality Agreements). Onalabs raises a NC form and/or CAPA if necessary.

If the test is passed, Oscatech delivers the necessary documentation to Onalabs Inno-Hub SL as accorded on the Quality Agreement (OLQMS-REC001.3 - Quality Agreements).

Manufacturing process on Oscatech facilities is described here:

Shipping to Onalabs

The finnished batch of ONAVITAL product is packaged by Oscatech as agreed on the Quality Agreement, found here: OLQMS-REC001.3 - Quality Agreements. The packaged batch will be sent to Onalabs for storage and distribution.

The inventory of every product received by Onalabs is carried out by Holded: https://app.holded.com/products
